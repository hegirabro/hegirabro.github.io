DOMINIC MATHEW
About me

Dominic is an architect-planner who works on addressing mobility and job-access challenges in Northeast Ohio with a venture philanthropy collaborative. He is interested in exploring the role of geospatial civic technology and social infrastructure in empowering underserved communities to participate in city planning processes. He is passionate about using digital engagement tools and open-access platforms to bridge the data science divide. Dominic has a Bachelor of Architecture from the Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra, India and a Master’s in Regional Planning with a focus on Environmental Finance and Impact Investing from Cornell University, Ithaca, USA.

Selected projects in chronological order

2020 Crowdsourcing Mobility Ideas in Northeast Ohio

This program tackles the region's 'no car, no job; no job, no car' paradox by testing innovative workforce and transportation solutions

2020 Scenario Modeling for Disruption due to COVID-19

Modelled unemployment insurance and demographic data to analyze industries most at risk of layoffs due to the pandemic in Northeast Ohio

2018 Myanmar’s Evolving Information Ecosystem

Streamlined service delivery for new municipal bodies using geospatial data and engaged with communities to sustain transparent interactions with the civic bodies

2017 Communications@CLASSE, Cornell

Assisted with communications and branding strategy at the Cornell Laboratory of Accelerator-Based Sciences and Education, a particle accelerator facility in the Cornell campus

2017 Calvert's Environmental Impacts and Outreach

Analyzed asset management company, Calvert's ESG performance as a Sustainable Global Enterprise fellow in the business school at Cornell

2016 Fairconditioning: Cooling Buildings Thoughtfully

An Indo-Swiss collaboration that aims at integrating sustainability in architecture and engineering curricula through interactive pedagogy

2015 Mapping Informal Transportation in Ranchi, India

A majority of trips in metropolitan Ranchi are done using informal transportation modes like auto and pedal powered rickshaws

2015 Community Engagement with Actionable Deliverables

Residents engaged in the co-creation of their neighborhoods in Ranchi, as part of my work at ITDP

2012 Urban Revitalization of Majnu ka Tilla

Re-imagining a Tibetan refugee community in New Delhi as part of my undergraduate architecture design thesis

2012 The BMW-Guggenheim Urban Lab in Berlin

Participated in this global moving lab for my first exposure to global urban design and planning trends
